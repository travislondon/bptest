Use Case Diagram

Many of the following definitions of were derived in part from descriptions and definitions in The Unified Modeling Language Reference Manual, Second Edition; and UML Distilled, Third Edition.

Use case diagrams describe relationships between and among use cases and between use cases and actors.  Use case diagrams are informal in the sense that they do not impose behavior on formal diagrams such as the instance state machine and class state machine diagrams.  This implementation of use case diagrams does not include an explicit subject drawing tool.  The subject of a use case diagram may be implied from where it is placed.  Use case diagrams may be placed in domain and subsystem packages.  They may also be nested inside other use case diagrams.

Actor

An actor is an abstraction of a role played by something external to the subject of the diagram.  The role may be played by an external user or by an element of the system.  Actors may be named. 

Graphical Notation

An actor is represented as a stick figure man symbol.  The name of the actor is displayed below the stick figure.

Use Case

A use case represents an interaction between a user of a system and the system itself.  The narrative of the use case may be placed in its description editor; which may accessed via its context menu.

Graphical Notation

A use case is represented on the use case diagram as an oval.  The name of the use case is displayed in the center of the oval.

Binary Association

An association abstracts the communication path between an actor and a use case.

Graphical Notation

An external entity is represented by thin solid line

Generalization Association

A generalization association abstracts the relationship between a general use case and more specific use case or a general and more specific actor.

Graphical Notation

A generalization association is represented by a thin solid line between two use cases or two actors.  It is terminated at the general end by a hollow triangle shape.

Include Relationship

An include relationship abstracts the insertion of additional behavior into a base use case that does not know about it.

Graphical Notation

An include relationship is represented by a thin dotted line between two use cases.  It is terminated at the additional behavior end with an open arrow head and decorated with the <<include>> stereotype.

Extend Relationship

An extend relationship abstracts the insertion of additional behavior into a base use case that explicitly describes the insertion.

Graphical Notation

An extend relationship is represented by a thin dotted line between two use cases.  It is terminated at the base case end with an open arrow head and decorated with the <<extend>> stereotype.
